# Trump on Inflation - ZDF Heute Show

## Introduction
Inflation and taxation have long been central themes in American economic debates, and Donald Trump has consistently tied the two together in his public remarks. Framing rising prices as a direct consequence of tax policies, he argues that burdensome taxation fuels economic strain for households and businesses alike. This perspective highlights his broader economic narrative: that lowering taxes is key to curbing inflation and stimulating growth.

- Short-term growth: The TCJA initially boosted GDP growth and corporate profits. Wages rose modestly, and unemployment fell to historic lows before the pandemic.
- Deficits: The tax cuts reduced federal revenue by an estimated $3 trillion over 10 years, increasing the national debt.
- Inflation link: Economists note that tax cuts increased disposable income and demand, but inflation in the U.S. was primarily driven by:
	- Pandemic-era supply chain disruptions.
	- Tariffs on imports (especially from China), which raised consumer prices.
	- Energy and housing costs.
	- Fiscal stimulus measures beyond Trump‚Äôs tax policies.
